# Communication Templates Guide

## Overview

Communication templates are pre-built message frameworks designed to streamline client and team communication throughout the visit lifecycle. They provide consistent messaging, ensure important details are communicated at the right time, and reduce manual composition effort.

Templates are available in the **Communications** section. Click **"Use Template"** on any template to customize and send it for a specific visit.

---

## The 6 Communication Templates

### 1. Initial Planning Email

**Channel:** Email
**Timing:** 10–14 days before arrival
**Recipient:** Client attendees

**Purpose:**
Kick off the visit planning process by gathering essential information from the client.

**Content includes:**
- Welcome message and visit overview
- Request for attendee details (names, roles, contact info)
- Flight information (airline, flight number, times)
- Hotel preferences and special requests
- Dietary requirements and accessibility needs
- Visit objectives and expected outcomes
- Request for any special requirements or sensitivities

**When to send:**
As soon as the visit date is confirmed. This provides the client 2 weeks to collect and respond with their information.

---

### 2. Confirmed Itinerary Email

**Channel:** Email
**Timing:** 5–7 days before arrival
**Recipient:** Client attendees

**Purpose:**
Provide confirmed logistics and schedule so clients have everything they need for their visit.

**Content includes:**
- Flight confirmation details (arrival date/time, airport, terminal)
- Driver information (name, contact, vehicle details, pickup location/time)
- Hotel confirmation (name, address, check-in/out dates, room details)
- Office location and address
- Parking and access instructions
- Full visit schedule (daily agenda, meeting times, attendees)
- Dress code and office policies
- Contact numbers for day-of support
- Wi-Fi and other facility information

**When to send:**
Once all logistics are finalized. This should be sent 5–7 days before arrival to allow time for the client to review and ask clarifying questions.

---

### 3. Day-Before Reminder

**Channel:** WhatsApp
**Timing:** 1 day before arrival
**Recipient:** Client attendees

**Purpose:**
Warm, personal check-in to confirm arrival details and provide immediate support contact.

**Content includes:**
- Friendly welcome message
- Confirmation of driver name and arrival details
- Driver contact number
- Primary support contact number
- Light, welcoming tone to build rapport

**When to send:**
Evening before arrival or morning of arrival. Keeps arrival logistics fresh in the client's mind and establishes personal connection.

---

### 4. Day-Of Arrival Check-In

**Channel:** WhatsApp
**Timing:** Day of arrival
**Recipient:** Client attendees

**Purpose:**
Ensure client is doing well upon arrival and provide immediate support if needed.

**Content includes:**
- Welcome message
- Confirmation that driver/support team is standing by
- Live support contact number
- Warm, supportive tone
- Acknowledgment of travel and any immediate needs

**When to send:**
Upon client arrival or shortly after they land. Demonstrates attentiveness and ensures they know how to reach support immediately.

---

### 5. Internal Team Brief

**Channel:** Slack
**Timing:** Before visit starts (typically 2–5 days before)
**Recipient:** Internal team (all staff involved in visit)

**Purpose:**
Align the internal team on visit objectives, schedule, and client expectations.

**Content includes:**
- Client company name and key attendees
- Visit objectives and expected outcomes
- Full daily schedule
- Client sensitivities or special requirements
- Dress code expectations
- Attendance expectations (who should attend which sessions)
- Key talking points or preparation needed
- Logistics overview (who's driving, who's greeting, etc.)
- Any dietary or accessibility requirements
- Emergency contacts and escalation procedures

**When to send:**
2–5 days before the visit. Gives the team time to prepare while keeping details fresh.

---

### 6. Post-Visit Thank You

**Channel:** Email
**Timing:** Within 2 business days of visit end
**Recipient:** Client attendees

**Purpose:**
Express gratitude, summarize what was accomplished, and confirm next steps.

**Content includes:**
- Warm thank you message
- Specific highlights of the visit (key discussions, agreements)
- Summary of outcomes and deliverables discussed
- Confirmed next steps and timelines
- Offer of continued support
- Request for feedback (optional survey link)
- Contact information for future questions

**When to send:**
Within 2 business days while the visit is fresh in everyone's mind. Demonstrates professionalism and maintains momentum for follow-up actions.

## How to Use Templates in the System

### Step-by-Step

1. Navigate to **Communications** in the main menu
2. Scroll to the **Communication Templates** section
3. Click **"Use Template"** on the desired template
4. A modal will open where you can:
   - **Select the visit** you want to send the communication for
   - **Enter the recipient** (email address, phone number, or contact name)
   - **Customize the subject line** (use +Company and +Dates buttons to insert dynamic placeholders)
   - **Add notes** or draft the full message if you want to personalize it
5. Click **"Create Communication"** to save as a draft
6. The communication will appear in:
   - The visit's **Communications** tab (for editing/sending later)
   - The global **Communication Log** on the Communications page

### Status and Next Steps

- Communications are saved as **Draft** by default
- You can edit them in the visit's Communications tab before sending
- Mark as **Sent** when you actually send the communication
- Set the **Date Sent** to track when the communication was delivered

## Best Practices

✓ **Do:**
- Send Initial Planning emails immediately after visit confirmation
- Customize the subject line with the client's company name
- Add any special notes in the Notes field (e.g., "Client is very formal - use professional tone")
- Log all communications, even if sent outside the system (for record-keeping)
- Review the template description before sending to ensure it matches your visit timeline

✗ **Don't:**
- Skip the follow-up thank you email — it maintains relationships
- Send Confirmed Itinerary emails before logistics are actually confirmed
- Use templates as exact text — adapt them to your client's style and needs
- Forget to set the date sent when logging past communications
